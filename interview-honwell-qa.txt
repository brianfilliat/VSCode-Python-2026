User: Safe rollout strategy: How do you roll out cookbook/policy changes safely in production? What promotion path do you use, how do you do canaries, what’s your rollback plan, and how do you avoid surprise changes?



1) Q: What promotion path is recommended for cookbook/policy changes?
A: Define scope and acceptance criteria, then promote through: dev → CI (unit + lint) → staging (integration + e2e) → canary → production, gating each step with automated checks and a human approver for production.

2) Q: How should canaries be run?
A: Route ~1–5% traffic or deploy to 1 host/cluster initially; target low-risk tenants/regions; run smoke and synthetic tests; observe for a short validation window (15–60m); use concrete failure thresholds to auto-fail.

3) Q: What validation is important during a canary?
A: Smoke tests, policy-specific functional tests, synthetic transactions, and observability for errors, latency, and business KPIs with automated alerts.

4) Q: What rollback mechanisms are recommended?
A: Fast flips via feature flags or config toggles, orchestration rollback (helm/terraform/ansible), a pre-baked runbook with exact commands and approvals, and post-rollback validation and postmortem.

5) Q: How do you avoid surprise changes in production?
A: Enforce change windows, require tickets/PRs/changelogs, use immutable artifacts, enable automated drift detection, and require protected-branch approvals for production.

6) Q: What operational controls should be in place?
A: SLOs with alerts, a canary dashboard comparing canary vs baseline metrics, audit logs, runbooks for common failures, and gradual ramping with validations at each step.

7) Q: What automation and CI safety practices were suggested?
A: Prefer feature flags for quick rollback; CI gates (lint, tests, policy lint); pre-deploy simulations or dry-runs; least-privilege promotions and documented runbooks.

8) Q: What CI workflow example was provided?
A: A GitHub Actions workflow `.github/workflows/cookbook-policy-ci.yml` with jobs: `validate` (lint/tests), `policy-lint`, `canary-deploy` (manual), and `promote-to-production` (environment protected).

9) Q: How should the workflow be used?
A: Commit the workflow, enforce branch + environment protections, replace placeholder deploy/lint commands with your scripts, and use the manual dispatch for canary/promote steps.

10) Q: What cloud architecture was described for an end-to-end SaaS platform?
A: Multi-tenant data-processing architecture on AWS: control-plane accounts via Organizations; EKS for microservices; Aurora (Postgres) Multi-AZ; S3; Kinesis/MSK; ElastiCache and DynamoDB for state; Secrets Manager/KMS; CI/CD with immutable artifacts.

11) Q: Key networking & identity decisions?
A: One VPC per account/environment with multiple AZs; Transit Gateway/VPC endpoints; private connectivity (PrivateLink); AWS SSO with corporate IdP and MFA; IAM roles and IRSA for fine-grained service identity.

12) Q: How was HA and DR designed?
A: Multi-AZ by default; multi-region active/passive DR with cross-region read replicas and S3 replication; DB snapshots/PITR; Route53 failover and regular DR rehearsals (target RTO/RPO defined).

13) Q: What canary deployment strategy was recommended?
A: Build immutable artifacts, progressive rollouts (1% → 5% → 25% → 100%) using rollout tooling (Argo Rollouts/Istio/ALB weights) and feature flags; rollback via flag flip or previous artifact.

14) Q: What Terraform starter scaffold was added by the assistant?
A: Starter files placed under `iac/terraform`: `main.tf`, `variables.tf`, `outputs.tf`, and `README.md` (to be customized with credentials and variable values).

15) Q: Any notes on git merging and pushing changes?
A: If push fails due to non-fast-forward, pull/rebase or fetch and merge remote `main` first; stash uncommitted work or commit before merging; resolve conflicts and then push.

16) Q: What next steps were offered in the session?
A: Create a templated canary checklist, a rollback playbook, sample smoke-test and policy-lint scripts, or commit/push the workflow and IaC files; the assistant offered to add files into the repo when requested.
